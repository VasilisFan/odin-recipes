Easy ice cube tray sushi

Description

Whip up this easy and original individual moussaka; a fun twist on the classic Greek comfort dish! Featuring a base of eggplant, a rich meat sauce filling, and a topping of bechamel and crispy potato chips, this recipe will amaze you!

Ingredients

For the eggplants

  • 3 eggplants, medium
  • 6 teaspoon(s) olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon(s) thyme, dried
  • salt
  • pepper

For the meat sauce

  • 400 g ground beef, chuck
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove(s) of garlic
  • 200 g zucchinis
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 tablespoon(s) olive oil
  • 40 g red wine
  • 400 g canned tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon(s) granulated sugar
  • 1 stick(s) cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon(s) thyme, dried
  • 50 g gruyere cheese, grated
  • salt
  • pepper

For the bechamel sauce

  • 50 g butter
  • 50 g all-purpose flour
  • 500 g whole milk, at room temperature
  • 50 g gruyere cheese, grated + extra 50 g for the baking
  • ¼ teaspoon(s) ground nutmeg
  • 2 egg yolks, from medium eggs
  • salt
  • pepper

For the potato chips

  • 1 potato
  • 1 liter sunflower oil, for the frying
  • salt

To serve

  • parsley, finely chopped

Execution Method

For the eggplants

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (390°F) set to fan.
  2. Line a baking pan with parchment paper and set it aside.
  3. Cut the eggplants lengthwise in half, keeping the stem intact.
  4. Score the inside of the eggplants in a crisscross pattern with a knife, being careful not to cut through the skin.
  5. Add the olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper, and spread them with your hands to marinate the inner side well.
  6. Place the eggplants on the baking pan, skin-side up, put the pan in the oven, and bake the eggplants for 40 minutes until tender. (At this point, you can prepare the meat sauce and béchamel.)
  7. Remove the pan from the oven and, if the meat sauce and béchamel are ready, increase the oven’s temperature to 220°C (430°F) set to broiler.
  8. Turn the eggplants skin-side down, gently press the flesh with a spoon to create space for the meat filling, and set them aside.

For the meat sauce

  1. Finely chop the onion, garlic, and parsley, cut the zucchini into small cubes, and set them aside.
  2. Place a non-stick frying pan over high heat, add the olive oil, and let it heat up well.
  3. Add the ground beef and sauté it for 3-4 minutes until nicely browned.
  4. Add the onion, zucchini, and garlic, mix with a wooden spoon, and keep sauteing for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Deglaze the pan with the wine and let its alcohol evaporate completely.
  6. Add the canned tomatoes, sugar, cinnamon stick, thyme, salt, and pepper, mix well, and cook the sauce for another 4-5 minutes.
  7. Remove the pan from the heat, add the parsley and gruyere cheese, and mix with a serving spoon.
  8. Fill the eggplants with the meat sauce and set them aside.

For the bechamel sauce

  1. Place a small pot over medium heat, add the butter cut into cubes, and let it melt.
  2. Add the flour and mix with a wooden spoon well for 1-2 minutes until they are completely combined and you have a smooth paste.
  3. Add the milk in 3-4 batches, whisking constantly until the mixture comes to a boil, thickens, and the first bubbles appear on the surface.
  4. Remove the pot from the heat, add the 50 g gruyere cheese, nutmeg, egg yolks, salt, and pepper, and whisk well until completely combined.
  5. Spoon about 3 tablespoons of bechamel sauce over each eggplant, and spread it carefully over the meat sauce.
  6. Sprinkle the remaining gruyere cheese over the bechamel sauce, put the pan in the oven, and bake for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Remove the pan from the oven.

For the decoration

  1. Peel the potato and cut it -from its large side- into very thin slices using a mandoline slicer.
  2. Stack the slices on top of each other and cut them into very thin sticks.
  3. Separate the sticks with your hands and transfer them to a bowl of water.
  4. Rinse very well to remove the starch, drain them well, and pat dry with paper towels.
  5. Place a deep frying pan over high heat, add the sunflower oil, and let it heat up well.
  6. Line a small baking pan with paper towels and set it aside.
  7. Lower the heat to medium, add the potato sticks to the pan in 2 batches, and fry them for 3 minutes until crispy.
  8. Transfer them to the baking pan using a slotted spoon, and remove the pan from the heat.
  9. Divide the potato chips over the eggplants.
  10. Sprinkle the chips with salt and serve with finely chopped parsley.
